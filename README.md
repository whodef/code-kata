## Description

As software developers, we all know how satisfying it can be to sharpen our skills and grow in our craft. But sometimes, the best way to level up isn't just through daily work — it's through deliberate practice. That’s where the concept of kata comes in.

In martial arts, a kata is a carefully choreographed pattern of movements, practiced alone or in unison with others, to build discipline and mastery. Sound familiar? In software development, we do something very similar. We write code, solve problems, and repeat patterns — sometimes solo, sometimes in teams, and often in pair programming sessions.

Think of coding kata as a way to exercise your programming muscles. It’s practice that’s focused, intentional, and yes — surprisingly fun. Whether you're flying solo or pairing up with a teammate, kata offer a structured way to explore new ideas, refine your technique, and become a better developer.
